Historical Trends

ARO Growth Over
5 Years

Understanding ARO trends helps shops benchmark performance and identify growth opportunities in an evolving market. The best auto repair software makes capturing that growth systematic rather than accidental.

2020

Industry Average: $340-$380

Pre-pandemic baseline. Many shops operated with paper inspections and phone-based approvals. Digital adoption was limited to early adopters and tech-forward markets.

2021

Industry Average: $355-$400

Pandemic accelerated digital adoption as shops sought contactless workflows. Early digital inspection adopters began seeing significant ARO separation from traditional shops.

2022

Industry Average: $370-$420

Parts inflation and vehicle complexity drove natural ARO growth. Digital inspection adoption reached 35-40% of shops, widening the performance gap.

2023

Industry Average: $380-$435

SMS approvals and declined work follow-up systems became mainstream. Shops with comprehensive digital workflows achieved 40-50% higher ARO than industry average.

2024

Industry Average: $390-$450

Digital inspection adoption exceeded 50%. Top-performing shops using photo evidence, SMS approvals, and automated follow-up consistently exceed $650 ARO.

Growth Drivers

What's Driving
ARO Increases

Specific technologies and processes are responsible for the majority of ARO growth across the industry.

01

Digital Inspections with Photo Evidence

Customers who see photographic evidence of repairs approve work at 2× the rate of those who only hear about it. This single technology drives 15-25% of ARO improvement for adopting shops.

02

SMS Estimate Approvals

84% of SMS estimates are approved same-day versus 35% for phone-based approvals. Faster approval cycles reduce customer hesitation and increase total approved work per visit.

03

Comprehensive Multi-Point Inspections

Digital inspection templates ensure every vehicle receives a thorough inspection every time. Consistency in identifying all necessary repairs directly increases ARO.

04

Declined Work Follow-Up Systems

Automated follow-up on declined repairs recovers 25-35% of deferred revenue within 30 days. This turns one-time visits into ongoing ARO opportunities.

Shop Type Comparison

ARO by
Shop Type

Different shop types naturally achieve different ARO levels due to specialization, customer base, and service mix.

DS

Dealership Service Centers: $450-$550

Higher parts margins, factory-trained technicians, and OEM requirements drive higher ARO. However, dealership adoption of digital inspections lags independent shops, limiting growth potential.

GR

General Repair Shops: $380-$450

Industry baseline. Shops here have significant growth opportunity through digital adoption. General shops that implement digital workflows often reach dealership-level ARO within 6-12 months.

SP

Specialty Shops: $500-$700

European import, diesel, transmission, and EV specialists command premium ARO due to specialized expertise, higher parts costs, and customers who expect and pay for premium service.

TC

Tire & Quick Service: $180-$280

Lower ARO due to transactional nature and limited service scope. These shops compensate with higher volume and faster turnaround. Digital adoption here focuses on speed and convenience.

ARO FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current industry average ARO?+

The national average ARO is $390-$450. However, shops using digital inspections with photo evidence consistently achieve $550-$650. Top-performing shops with comprehensive digital workflows regularly exceed $650 ARO.

How has ARO changed over the past 5 years?+

Industry ARO has increased approximately 15-20% over the past 5 years, driven by vehicle complexity, parts inflation, and wider adoption of digital inspection tools. Shops that embraced digital workflows saw 25-35% ARO growth compared to 10-15% for traditional shops.

What factors are driving ARO increases?+

Primary drivers include digital inspections with photo evidence (increases approval rates by 2×), SMS estimate approvals (84% same-day approval rate), declined work follow-up systems, and comprehensive multi-point inspections that identify all necessary repairs.

How does ARO vary by shop type?+

Dealership service centers average $450-$550 ARO due to higher parts margins and factory-trained technicians. General repair shops average $380-$450. Specialty shops (European, diesel, transmission) average $500-$700 due to specialized expertise and higher parts costs.
