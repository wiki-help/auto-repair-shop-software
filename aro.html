The ARO System

Four Steps to a
Higher Ticket

ARO improvement doesn't require hiring a slicker salesperson. It requires a repeatable process that informs customers rather than pressures them.

01

Digital multi-point inspection on every car

Every vehicle gets a full inspection, every finding is documented with photos or video. This is the foundation — nothing else works without it.

02

Send findings via SMS with photo evidence

Text the customer a link to their inspection report with photos while they wait. Customers who see a cracked belt or worn brake pad at 2mm approve the work at 2× the rate of customers who only hear about it.

03

Make same-day approval effortless

Every finding in the digital report should have a one-tap approval button. Remove every obstacle between 'I see the problem' and 'yes, fix it'. 84% of SMS estimates are approved same-day.

04

Tag and follow up on every declined item

Every approval that doesn't happen today is deferred revenue. Log it, set a follow-up date, and automate the reminder sequence. That's your ARO continuing to work after the car leaves.

ARO Questions

ARO FAQ

What is a good ARO for an auto repair shop?+

The industry average sits between $380 and $450. Shops using digital multi-point inspections with photo evidence consistently reach $550–$650. Shops that also implement SMS estimate approvals and declined work follow-up regularly exceed $650.

Is raising ARO the same as upselling?+

No. Upselling means recommending work the car doesn't need. Raising ARO means ensuring customers are fully aware of every legitimate issue found during inspection, and making it easy for them to approve the work they already need. The difference is transparency and evidence.

How quickly can I increase my ARO?+

Shops that implement digital inspections typically see ARO improvement within the first 2–3 weeks. The biggest single impact is sending photo or video evidence of findings — approval rates jump from ~35% to ~65% almost immediately.
