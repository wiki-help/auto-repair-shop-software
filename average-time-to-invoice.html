Where Time Is Lost

The Four Biggest
Invoice Delays

Most of the time between wrench-down and invoice is administrative, not mechanical. The best auto repair software eliminates these four bottlenecks through digital workflows and automated approvals.

47min

Waiting for Customer Approval

Phone tag is the biggest single delay. Shops still using phone calls for estimate approvals wait an average of 47 minutes. SMS approval drops this to 11 minutes — a 77% reduction with no change to the repair itself.

38min

Parts Procurement Gaps

Disorganized parts ordering, supplier delays, and poor inventory management cause significant mid-RO wait times. Shops with integrated parts ordering and real-time availability data cut this delay by 50-60%.

22min

Technician-to-Advisor Communication

Walking back and forth between the shop floor and the service desk, or waiting to verbally relay inspection findings, wastes significant time. Digital inspection platforms eliminate most of this communication overhead.

18min

Manual Invoice Creation

Manually entering labor times, parts, and notes from a paper RO adds unnecessary time at the end of every job. Digital workflows that auto-populate the invoice from the inspection and approved estimate cut this to under 4 minutes.

Throughput Impact

What Faster Invoicing
Does to Revenue

Reducing time-to-invoice isn't just an operational improvement — it directly translates to more vehicles per day and more revenue per bay.

01

More Vehicles Per Bay Per Day

Cutting 1 hour of administrative time per RO on a 10-bay shop creates the effective capacity of 1.5 additional bays — without any capital investment in new bays or equipment.

02

Improved Cash Flow

Every hour an invoice sits unclosed is a delay to payment. Shops that invoice faster collect faster, improving cash position and reducing the receivables cycle by an average of 1.2 days.

03

Higher Customer Satisfaction

Customers who receive their vehicle on time or early give 4.7× more 5-star reviews than customers who wait longer than expected. Time-to-invoice is one of the most direct drivers of customer experience.

04

Reduced Technician Idle Time

When parts, approvals, and communication are slow, technicians wait. Faster workflows reduce tech idle time by 15-25%, directly improving productivity metrics without scheduling changes.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good time-to-invoice benchmark for auto repair?+

Industry average is 3.2-4.5 hours from check-in to invoice. Top-performing shops achieve 2.1-2.8 hours through digital inspections, SMS estimate approvals, and streamlined parts ordering. Every hour saved translates directly to more vehicles per bay per day.

What causes delays between repair completion and invoicing?+

The biggest delays are waiting for customer approval (avg 47 minutes), parts procurement (avg 38 minutes), technician-to-advisor communication gaps (avg 22 minutes), and manual invoice creation (avg 18 minutes). Digital workflows address all four.

How does faster invoicing affect shop revenue?+

Reducing time-to-invoice by 1 hour on a 10-bay shop averaging 3 vehicles/bay/day frees up the equivalent of 1.5 additional bays worth of capacity. For a shop with a $280 average ARO, that's approximately $25,000-$35,000 in additional monthly revenue potential.

What is the fastest way to reduce time-to-invoice?+

SMS estimate approval is the single highest-impact change — it reduces customer approval wait time from 47 minutes (phone tag) to 11 minutes average. Combined with digital inspections that auto-populate the estimate, shops can cut total time-to-invoice by 35-45%.
