Maps Ranking System

Three Factors That
Control Your Ranking

Google's local algorithm uses three primary signals. You can't control proximity (how close you are to the searcher), but you can fully control the other two.

01

Relevance — Does your profile match the search?

Select every applicable service category. Write detailed descriptions for each service. Add photos of each service type. Google needs to understand exactly what you offer.

02

Prominence — How well-known is your business?

Reviews are the biggest prominence signal. Response rate matters too — shops that respond to every review (including 1-stars) rank higher than those that ignore them.

03

Enable direct booking with Reserve with Google

A 'Book Online' button directly in your Maps listing removes all friction. Customers who can book without calling convert at a dramatically higher rate, especially after hours.

04

Post weekly updates to your GBP

Active profiles rank higher. Post a promotion, a completed job photo, or a seasonal tip every 7 days. Each post signals to Google that your business is current and engaged.
