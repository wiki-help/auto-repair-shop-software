Top Missed Opportunities

What Shops Most
Commonly Miss

These five categories appear on most vehicles and are consistently underreported during inspections. The best auto repair software surfaces them automatically during digital inspections so advisors never miss the prompt.

01

Cabin & Engine Air Filters

Present on 100% of vehicles, checked on fewer than 40% of ROs. Average ticket addition of $25-$65 with 60-70% gross margin. The single easiest revenue addition in the shop.

02

Coolant & Transmission Fluid Service

Interval-based services that most shops fail to proactively recommend. Shops with systematic maintenance tracking capture these on 3× more vehicles than shops relying on advisor memory.

03

Wiper Blades

Checked on fewer than 25% of ROs despite being a high-visibility safety item. Quick to inspect, quick to replace, and customers appreciate the recommendation — especially before winter or rainy seasons.

04

Tire Rotation & Inspection

Missed on 35-45% of eligible vehicles. Beyond the rotation revenue, a proper tire inspection surfaces alignment, suspension, and brake wear that drives additional high-value work.

05

Declined Work Follow-Up

60-70% of declined repairs are never followed up on. Shops with automated follow-up at 7, 30, and 90 days recover 28-35% of declined work, adding $15,000-$40,000/month for a 10-bay shop.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most commonly missed upsell in auto repair?+

Cabin air filters are the single most commonly missed upsell — present in nearly every vehicle and often overlooked during inspection. They take 5 minutes to replace and carry 60-70% gross margin. Shops that check them consistently add $18-$25 average to every RO.

How much revenue does a shop lose from missed upsells annually?+

Shops averaging 15 ROs/day that miss $180 in upsells per RO lose approximately $985,000 in annual revenue. Even recovering 30% of missed opportunities adds $295,000/year to revenue for a shop that size.

Do digital inspections actually improve upsell capture?+

Yes — significantly. Shops using digital inspections with photo and video evidence see 2× higher approval rates on recommended services. Customers who can see the problem are far more likely to approve the repair, particularly for items like dirty cabin filters, worn belts, and brake wear.

How do you train service advisors to present upsells effectively?+

The highest-performing advisors present every recommended service with the photo or video from the inspection, explain the consequence of deferring, and give a clear price. Training advisors to use this three-part framework consistently increases upsell capture by 40-60%.
